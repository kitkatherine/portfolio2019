DHL

International Landing Pages

Competitive landing pages designed to A/B tested with DHL's previously launched campaigns in Mexico, France, and the United States.

Launch Project

BACKGROUND

DHL was launching new paid search campaigns across multiple regions. They wanted landing pages that reflected their brand guidelines and used data from their previous campaigns to inform strategy on new pages.

GOAL

Each of these pages were A/B tested against a landing page in ion already running. The goal was a lift in conversions (form completion) and reduction in bounce rate when compared to the competitor.

TIMELINE

These projects were strategized, protyped, designed/developed, and launched in just over 45 days.

DHL Mexico

Our team, comprised of our content strategist, UX designer, and data analyst, reviewed the analytics from the previous landing page. We realized a low number of form completions, and overall low engagement with the page. We brought in some videos, testimonials, and the focus of our strategy was on the form. By splitting the form into three steps with graphic segmentations, we hoped to lower cognitive load on first impression. We also lowered the amount of form fields to only necessary fields.

DHL France

The previous page had a long form, little information, and message match both with their paid search ads and audience. Our content strategiest took a look into the language on their paid search ads, and we developed a strategy around a better message match and getting that message prominently featured on the page. We also aimed to fix a few UX issues, such as a situation where if the user filled out the form and selected "Personal", half of the form fields didn't apply to them and were thrown out as invalid.

DHL USA

Once the template needs were defined, each month priorities were set and myself and my team met to determine needs for each. Utlizing a project template I created in Google Drive, we would establish necessary user experience level, purpose, expected content (eg: a yearly report for an infographic), conversion point, value, and necessary interactive elements. From these guidelines, a quick sketch would be created to establish quick agreement on the design and layout before jumping into creating a low fidelity prototype in the ion interactive platform.

The ion interactive platform itself allowed us to create designs and prototypes quickly - sometimes in an hour or two - before diving into the full fledged design and development of a final template. This gave us the unique ability to iterate and test a template quickly.

Results

In Mexico, we lowered form dropdoff by 5% and successfully split off personal customers before collecting their lead information, garnering more qualified leads. There was a 48% increase in page conversions and our challenger was declared the winner over the control page. In France, we had a 27% increase in lead converstion rate and another winner over the control at 99% statistical confidence. In USA, we saw 35% of respondents convert and a lower overall bounce rate when compared to the control.
