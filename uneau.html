University of New England, Australia

Work Predictor Tool

A look at over 3,000 job titles and how AI or robotics will affect the tasks related to these jobs.

Launch Project

BACKGROUND

UNE had a vague idea of a tool they wanted, and came to ion to build out a RFP bidding against other competitors. Marketing technologist Katie Dick and I worked together to create a Scope of Work defining the user journey, technical requirements, and limitations. We were chosen to complete the final design.

GOAL

Creating an easy, user-friendly way to view hundreds of pages of research data. A user should be able to get to the first details of their expected job changes in less than 4 clicks.

TIMELINE

From request to final launch, this project took place over 6 months, with the majority of that being in the request and planning phases.

Challenges

This project stretched the capabilities of the ion interactive platform and allowed both Katie and I to stretch our technical muscles to develop a truly custom solution for UNE that remained easily editable by their marketing team. For their marketing team, we needed to ensure the experience was viewable and editable in the platform. For the end user, we needed a seamless experience that was simplistic, easy to use, and gained interest in the university itself.

Concept Development

Utilizing our knowledge from creating similar experiences, I strategized some initial concepts and created a few as low fidelity wireframes. Our creative director and marketing technologist reviewed, and with their input, I created a high fidelity wireframe with details for the UNE team to review to accompany our quote. Once approved, we worked with the UNE team to iron out design changes, any technical questions (such as how to deal with grammatical errors when their data was dynamically inserted), and establish a final plan for development of the project.

Development

Working with marketing technologist Katie Dick, I created a shell with input fields and design as she implemented the neccessary data import and Javascript codes. Using Slack and LiquidPlanner, we kept in touch often regarding project milestones and were able to collaborate in real time, sharing screens when necessary.

Launch and Updates

A design is never complete, they say. Upon launching, UNE saw more mobile traffic that intially expected. From their feedback, I worked on additional changes to the UI to improve the mobile user experience. This included enlarging search fields and icons, adjusting text and headlines for mobile users, and additional, extensive mobile browser testing.
