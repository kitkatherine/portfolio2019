Quick Start Cloud Templates

Management and design of over 100 templates over 5 years at SaaS company ion interactive.

Launch Project

BACKGROUND

The ion interactive platform relies on over 100 templates to create data-driven experiences without tech-savvy resources or developer code. The platform shifted to this templated approach in 2014 when I joined, and a major part of my role was developing these templates.

GOAL

Creating headache-free templates for web experiences that typically would require intensive front-end and back-end development, such as calculators or assessments. The templates needed to to be user-friendly to both the end-user and the customer utilizing the platform.

TIMELINE

The Quick Start Cloud has been a growing, iterative process that has been ongoing with myself and my team since 2014.

Challenges

In 2014, interactive content was beginning to come into the marketing technology world. At the time, Chief MarTec author Scott Brinker had coined the term 'marketing apps' as an emerging category, which the ion interactive software fit into. To differentiate the software both from competition and from it's previous iteration as landing page creation software, the Quick Start Cloud was born. Upon entering ion interactive in March of 2014, I was partnered with the company owner's, creative director, and VP of engineering to design and develop templates that fit the growing needs of our users.

Research

Starting out, our market was new, exciting, and relatively unknown. Through research conducted through Demand Metric, we discovered early needs for our marketing customers to be focused around assessments, infographics, and white papers. As the process evolved, the needs came from everywhere. From taking in requests from chat support tickets to data pulled from interactions with the current templates, the needs were reviewed on a monthly basis and prioritized as a team.

Ideation and Design

Once the template needs were defined, each month priorities were set and myself and my team met to determine needs for each. Utlizing a project template I created in Google Drive, we would establish necessary user experience level, purpose, expected content (eg: a yearly report for an infographic), conversion point, value, and necessary interactive elements. From these guidelines, a quick sketch would be created to establish quick agreement on the design and layout before jumping into creating a low fidelity prototype in the ion interactive platform.

The ion interactive platform itself allowed us to create designs and prototypes quickly - sometimes in an hour or two - before diving into the full fledged design and development of a final template. This gave us the unique ability to iterate and test a template quickly.

Creation and Development

Creating templates that aim to be a positive user experience both for the platform customer and the platform customer's end-user is an intricate challenge. The need for a beautiful, dynamic, and modern user experience is often hindered by the constraints of a platform-driven environment, the required effort of the platform's development team to make updates, and the platform user's ability with this type of software. Our team developed a detailed list of requirements for each template, ensuring that when a client chose a template, they could easily switch to their brand's CSS and have minimal updating to do for design.
